I had my very first coffee chat today-!! 😄

My guest was **Young Han**, a Korean international student YouTuber I’ve been following and supporting for a long time. I was deeply impressed by his determination to pursue his dreams with passion despite uncertainty. Now, he is working at **Meta** as a Partner Engineer in Generative AI.

Ever since I decided to build a career in the tech industry, I’ve held onto a few core goals:

- Work at a leading global company
- Develop products and services that deeply impact people’s daily lives
- Keep learning and growing at the forefront of technological change
- Be recognized not only in Korea but also in the United States as a professional

Recently, with the rise of concerns about developers being replaced by AI, I added another goal to ensure competitiveness 10–20 years from now:  
**transitioning from a full-stack web developer to an AI engineer.**

Because Young Han is living the life I aspire to, I was truly grateful that he accepted my coffee chat invitation. Despite my nervousness, the one-hour conversation was incredibly enjoyable.

Currently, I’m fortunate to have been admitted to a U.S. graduate program (MSCS-AI) and received my visa, but I’ve been worried about lacking project experience, making a career transition, and even feeling imposter syndrome, to the point of fearing burnout before I even begin.

That’s when Young Han delivered a very clear message:  
**“Build an overwhelming strength.”**

If the AI job market is tough and oversaturated, then **the only way to survive is to become overwhelmingly different from everyone else.**

---

## Key Advice from Young Han

He also gave me many actionable pieces of advice. Here are a few that stood out:

1. Learn the technologies of your target companies and build open-source projects using them.
2. CV and interview prep are basic — build overwhelming skills on top of them.
3. Get hands-on with AI agents, LangChain, and its sub-libraries (LangGraph, LangSmith).
4. When starting with any open source, read the entire official documentation and master it thoroughly.
5. A developer’s future competitiveness will depend on how well they leverage AI code generation tools.
6. Master Cursor — be able to fix every error AI-generated code throws at you.
7. Above all, don’t act out of pressure; pursue what excites you, and execute overwhelmingly.
8. Don’t go alone — work with outstanding people.

Thanks to these specific pieces of advice, I’ve shifted from feeling lost to seeing new possibilities, and I’m ready to begin preparing again with renewed motivation.
